<img src="https://images.theconversation.com/files/725731/original/file-20260324-71-h4hlss.gif" width="100" align="left">
The flagship outreach effort of our
<a href="http://goto-observatory.org">Gravitational-wave Optical Transient
Observer (GOTO)</a> project is 
<a
href="https://www.zooniverse.org/projects/tkillestein/kilonova-seekers">Kilonova
Seekers</a>, a project built on the Zooniverse platform which invites
members of the public to identify and confirm transients detected in GOTO
observations.
</p><p>
The project, brainchild of
<a href="https://tkilleste.in/about/">Tom Killestein (Warwick)</a> and
<a href="https://www.kicc.cam.ac.uk/staff/dr-lisa-kelsey">Lisa Kelsey
(Cambridge)</a>
was selected as one of "5 projects that made a difference" for a new 
<a
href="https://theconversation.com/what-weve-learned-from-citizen-science-5-projects-that-made-a-difference-279096">Conversation
article</a> published today. The article is part of a 
<a
href="https://theconversation.com/topics/citizen-science-series-186233">broader
series</a>
covering the wonderful world of citizen science.
</p>
